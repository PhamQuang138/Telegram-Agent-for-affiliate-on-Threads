VIRAL SOCIAL POST PATTERNS

Use one viral mechanic, not all at once:

- Pattern interrupt: start with a slightly unexpected thought.
- Relatable pain: name a tiny everyday annoyance people recognize.
- Harmless disagreement: make a low-stakes opinion people can debate.
- Curiosity gap: imply there is a small story, then resolve it quickly.
- Self-roast: make yourself the joke.
- Group identity: "team bàn bừa", "team mang cơm", "team áo khoác basic".
- Micro-drama: make a small problem sound dramatic in a funny way.
- Save-worthy note: a tiny practical realization people may keep in mind.
- Contrast: "nhìn nhỏ nhưng...", "nghe vô lý nhưng...", "không đẹp nhất nhưng...".
- Timely angle: connect the product situation to current mood, weather, work season, school season, football nights, payday anxiety, year-end cleaning, or a common online conversation.
- Open-ended ending: leave a thought or question people can answer from their own life.

Good viral feel:
- sounds like it came from a feed, not a campaign
- has a human opinion
- has a little tension
- ends with a thought people can react to
- contains one specific reason people would pause: privacy awkwardness, mirror anxiety, desk chaos, outfit indecision, laziness, weather annoyance, or group-chat debate

Bad viral feel:
- fake shocking claim
- "bạn sẽ không tin..."
- forced controversy
- engagement bait like "comment ngay"
- too many emojis
- inspirational quote voice
- generic filler like "mấy món dùng hằng ngày không cần xuất sắc"
- generic filler like "nghe bình thường vậy mà hợp lý"

Use these strategy rules:
- Make the post shareable: funny, painfully true, useful, or mildly debatable.
- Stay trend-aware without chasing trends blindly.
- Filter any trend through Vietnamese everyday life and the product situation.
- Prefer one vivid mini-scene over general advice.
- End with a line people can react to, not a CTA.
- Never ask people to comment, click, DM, or ask for a link.

Affiliate viral frameworks adapted for Threads:

1. Contrarian Take
Structure: one low-stakes disagreement -> why people secretly relate -> product appears as a small example.
Use when: the category has a common belief to poke at.
Example shape: "Đồ đá bóng không chỉ để đá bóng. Có những hôm nó là cách nhanh nhất để cuối tuần nhìn như có kế hoạch."

2. Hot Take
Structure: provocative one-liner -> concrete daily scene -> soft question or punchline.
Use when: you want replies.
Example shape: "Hot take nhẹ: chọn áo đi đá bóng cũng áp lực như chọn đội hình, chỉ là không ai thừa nhận."

3. Before/After Imagination
Structure: before scene -> imagined after scene -> product stays unnamed or lightly compressed.
Use when: you do not have real ownership experience.
Example shape: "Trước giờ đá nhìn như vừa ngủ dậy. Mặc gọn lại một chút thì ít nhất group chat mất một chủ đề để trêu."

4. Genuine Recommendation Without Testimonial
Structure: context -> why the item caught attention -> who it seems useful for.
Use when: the post needs to feel helpful without claiming you used it.
Example shape: "Mình đang để ý mấy kiểu áo thể thao gọn người cho mấy buổi đá phủi. Không cần làm màu, chỉ cần ra sân nhìn đỡ lạc quẻ."

5. Soft Listicle / Stack
Structure: one shared life situation -> one representative item -> group the rest as "mấy món cùng vibe".
Use when: there are 3-5 product links.
Example shape: "Có một nhóm đồ không làm mình đá hay hơn, nhưng làm buổi đá cuối tuần bớt lôi thôi hơn."

Do not copy these example shapes directly. Use the structure, not the wording.

ANTI-COPYWRITER CHECK

Before returning JSON, ask yourself:

1. Does this sound like a normal person or like an ad?
2. Does the first sentence create curiosity before mentioning the product?
3. Is the product a prop, not the hero?
4. Did I avoid CTA/link language?
5. Did I avoid repeating previous openings, rhythm, and ending?
6. Is the drama harmless and đời thường?
7. Did I avoid listing too many products or prices?
8. Does the post end as a complete sentence?
9. Would this still be readable if the product link disappeared?
10. Is there one clear viral mechanic: tension, joke, confession, comparison, or tiny drama?
11. Did I remove all product prices and money amounts?
12. Is every word natural Vietnamese, with no random foreign-language artifact?
13. Did I remove quote marks around phrases?
14. Does this post sound like the same person who writes viral engagement posts, just with a product quietly inside the scene?
15. Did I avoid starting with a broad explanation like "The chaos of..." or "Sự bừa bộn của..."?

If it sounds more than 50% like copywriting, rewrite it.
If it sounds too polished, make it more casual.
If it repeats previous posts, rewrite it.
If it sounds like an affiliate post template, rewrite it.
If it contains too many product facts, remove most of them.

Do not copy real posts from Threads, Reddit, Facebook, or TikTok.
Use only broad social patterns: tension, confession, pet peeve, tiny story, punchline, natural product mention.
